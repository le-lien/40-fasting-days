DAY 40 – STRATEGIC PRAYER MISSION

Priesthood – You are called to bear God’s presence

> “But you are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, His own special people, that you may proclaim the praises of Him who called you out of darkness into His marvelous light” (1 Peter 2:9)



> “He and has made us kings and priests to His God and Father, to Him be glory and dominion forever and ever. Amen.” (Revelation 1:6)



Today the Holy Spirit takes us back to the source of our ministry. Before we are intercessors, evangelists, prophets, teachers or leaders, we are priests of God. The priesthood is not a title or position. It's a way of life. A priest lives in God's presence, carries His heart, hears His voice and brings heaven to earth.

In the Old Testament, a priest's day began not with work, but with an encounter with God. Before he served the people, he stood before the Lord. Before he spoke, he listened. Before he helped people, he had himself purified. This is God's order to this day. Our service must never become greater than our fellowship with Jesus. Everything we share must first be received in His presence.

The temple shows us the path of a priest. The altar of burnt offering reminds us to give our own lives to Jesus daily. Pride, self-will and self-control die there. The pool of water reminds us that God's Word cleanses and renews our hearts daily. The table of showbread speaks of communion with Jesus - we do not live by our own strength, but by the bread of life. The golden lampstand reminds us that we are to be constantly filled with the Holy Spirit. The incense altar shows the life of intercession and worship. There our prayers rise like incense to God. The Holy of Holies is the place of deepest communion with the Father. That is where His glory resides. Today our heart is this place. Through Jesus we can enter God’s presence at any time.

A priest therefore not only lives in the temple - he becomes the temple himself. Everywhere you go, you carry God's presence within you. Your workplace becomes a temple. Your family becomes a temple. Your prayer room becomes a temple. Your everyday life becomes a church service. Every encounter becomes an opportunity to make Jesus visible.

As a prayer team, you are called to live this priesthood together. You stand up for people. You bring praise before God's throne. You pray for healing, deliverance and restoration. You proclaim truth. You help the weak. You strengthen the weary. You are bridges between heaven and earth. However, your most important service is not to do something for God, but to live with God.

Check your heart today. Do I seek God’s presence first or results first? Is my altar still burning? Does God’s Word cleanse me daily? Am I a person of intercession? Is Jesus really the center of my heart? Do I serve out of love or duty? Am I living holy because I love God, or just because I want to follow rules? Am I carrying God’s peace wherever I go? Do people recognize the character of Jesus through my life?

Today, intentionally take time to spiritually review each area of ​​the temple. Recommit your life to the altar. Ask God to cleanse your heart. Sit at the Lord's table and enjoy His fellowship. Ask the Holy Spirit to refill your candlestick with oil. Remain in prayer at the altar of incense. Enter the Holy of Holies in faith and spend time in the presence of your Father. Only then go out and serve people. A priest doesn't just bring words - he brings God's presence.

🙏 Prayer

**Lord Jesus Christ, You are my High Priest and the King of my life. I thank You for calling me to be a royal priest. Teach me to live daily in Your presence and make my heart Your temple. Purify me with Your Word, fill me anew with Your Holy Spirit and light the fire on my altar. Let my prayer life deepen, my love for you grow and my heart stay ever closer to yours. Make me a priest who brings hope, releases healing, advocates for others, lives truth and carries Your glory. Let our prayer team stand together as a royal priesthood so that Your Kingdom will be visible and many people will encounter Jesus. Your kingdom come. Thy will be done. Let your glory be seen – in our lives and through our ministry. In the name of Jesus. Amen.
