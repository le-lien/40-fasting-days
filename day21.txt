YOUR ASSIGNMENT FOR DAY 21

Today is about getting to know the Father—not as people, religion, or your past experiences have portrayed Him, but as Jesus revealed Him.

Since the Fall, the enemy has sought to distort the image of the Father. He wants people to see God as harsh, distant, unapproachable, or constantly angry. But Jesus came to reveal the Father. He said:

> “Anyone who has seen Me has seen the Father.” (John 14:9)



The Father was never against humanity. From the very beginning, He sought relationship. He walked with Adam and Eve in the Garden of Eden. He made a covenant with Abraham. He spoke with Moses face to face, as a friend speaks with a friend. He faithfully led Israel through the wilderness. Again and again, He sent prophets. Again and again, He called His people back—not because He wanted control, but because He desired relationship.

Yet death, sin, and separation became the greatest barrier between God and mankind. No sacrifice, no law, and no human effort could permanently remove that separation. Therefore, the Father gave the most precious gift He had—His own Son, Jesus Christ. Through the Cross, the way back to the Father was opened once and for all.

Today, your goal is not simply to know God as Creator or Judge, but to know Him as your Heavenly Father.


---

📖 1. Discover the Father's Heart

Read the following passages today:

Genesis 3

Genesis 17

Exodus 33

Luke 15

John 14–17

Romans 8

1 John 3


As you read, do not focus first on the people in the stories. Focus on the Father.

Ask yourself:

What moves His heart?

Why does He act this way?

What does He truly desire?

How does He reveal Himself?

What characteristics do I discover about Him?



---

❤️ 2. Don't Compare the Father to Earthly Fathers

Many people project their experiences with their earthly fathers onto God.

Ask the Holy Spirit to break every false image you have of the Father.

Write down:

What qualities do I believe God has?

Which of those come directly from Scripture?

Which have been shaped by wounds, disappointments, fear, or rejection?


Replace every lie with God's truth.


---

👑 3. See the Difference

Do not compare the Father to the gods of religions or mythology.

Other gods demand sacrifices.

The Father gave His own sacrifice.

Other gods seek to be served.

The Father seeks relationship.

Other gods rule through fear.

The Father gives acceptance.

Other gods demand performance.

The Father gives grace.

Other gods remain distant.

The Father comes near.

Write down every difference you discover.


---

⚔️ 4. Spiritual Warfare – Fight from the Identity of a Child

The enemy wants to convince you that God is against you. He wants to fill your heart with distrust, fear, shame, and distance from your Father.

Declare aloud today:

My Father is for me and not against me.

I am a beloved child of God.

Through Jesus, I have free access to the Father.

No accusation can separate me from His heart.

I do not have to earn His love.

I am welcome in His presence.

My Father knows me, leads me, and provides for me.


Every thought that pulls you away from the Father is not in agreement with His heart.


---

🙏 5. Spend Time with Your Father

Set aside at least 30 minutes today.

Do not begin by asking for answers or miracles.

Simply spend time with your Father.

Talk with Him.

Ask Him:

Father, who are You really?

How do You see me?

What do You want to reveal about Your heart today?

Where have I misunderstood You?

What do You want to restore between us?


Listen carefully and write down what God reveals to you through His Word and through the Holy Spirit.


---

✍️ 6. Write a Letter from the Father's Perspective

Imagine your Heavenly Father writing you a personal letter.

What truths would He speak over your life?

What fears would He remove?

What promises would He remind you of?

Write this letter using Scripture as your foundation, allowing God's Word to shape your heart.


---

🏆 GOAL OF DAY 21

The goal of today is to stop seeing the Father through the lens of your past, your experiences, or religious traditions, and instead to see Him through Jesus Christ.

The Father never walked away from humanity—it was humanity that walked away from Him. Since the Garden of Eden, the Father has been seeking His children. His greatest desire has always been relationship. That is why He sent Jesus: so that every barrier could be removed and we could once again come into His presence as sons and daughters.

The more you come to know the Father, the more you will discover that His nature is filled with love, holiness, truth, grace, and faithfulness. He is not looking for servants—He is looking for sons and daughters. He does not merely want to be worshiped; He wants to walk with you, lead you, and enjoy a personal relationship with you.
