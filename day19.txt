 DAY 19 – GET TO KNOW JESUS

Discover His Heart, His Nature, and His Character

> “Now this is eternal life: that they know You, the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom You have sent.” (John 17:3)



📖 INTRODUCTION

Everyone has a picture of who Jesus is. That picture is often shaped by our upbringing, sermons, books, or the opinions of other people. But God does not want us to know Jesus only through someone else's experience. He invites us to know Him personally.

Christianity is not primarily about rules, knowledge, or religious activities. It begins with a relationship with a Person—Jesus Christ. Therefore, the goal of our faith is not simply to know more facts about Jesus, but to know Him more deeply every day.

The more we know Jesus, the more we understand the heart of God. We discover what God is truly like. We see His love, His patience, His righteousness, His holiness, and His mercy. We learn how He treated people, how He cared for the weak, how He forgave, how He spoke the truth with love, and how He responded in every situation.

When we truly know Jesus, it becomes easier to trust Him. When we trust Him, we are willing to obey His Word. As we spend time with Him, we gradually become more like Him. His thoughts begin to shape our thinking. His love transforms our hearts. His humility changes the way we treat other people.

Jesus is the perfect example of a life fully surrendered to the Father. If we want to know how God thinks, how God loves, or how God acts, we only need to look at Jesus.

That is why today is not primarily about your problems or your needs. Today is about Jesus Himself.

🎯 YOUR ASSIGNMENT FOR TODAY

Today, spend intentional time reading one or more of the Gospels (Matthew, Mark, Luke, or John). Do not read simply to finish chapters. Instead, pray:

“Lord, show me who You really are.”

As you read, pay close attention to Jesus and ask yourself these questions:

How does Jesus treat people?

To whom does He give His attention?

How does He speak to children, the sick, sinners, and those rejected by society?

When does He show compassion?

When does He rejoice?

When does He weep?

When does He become angry, and why?

What moves His heart?

What matters most to Him?

What qualities do I see in His character?

What can I learn from Him today?


Write down every characteristic of Jesus that you discover. You may notice that He is patient, gentle, courageous, merciful, righteous, faithful, humble, loving, wise, compassionate, truthful, or full of grace. The more you study His nature, the more you will understand why people were drawn to Him.

Afterward, spend time in prayer thanking Jesus for what He has revealed to you. Ask the Holy Spirit to help you become more like Him. Pray honestly:

“Jesus, which part of Your character do You want to grow in my life today?”

Then put it into practice. Choose at least one characteristic of Jesus to live out today. Be patient with someone. Forgive someone. Listen carefully. Serve someone without expecting anything in return. Show mercy and compassion just as Jesus showed mercy and compassion to others.

🏆 GOAL OF DAY 19

The goal of today is not simply to gain more information about Jesus, but to know Him personally.

The more we know Jesus, the more we understand the Father. The more we discover His heart, the more our own hearts are transformed. We learn not only what Jesus did, but why He did it. As we know Him more deeply, our faith grows, our trust becomes stronger, and our love for Him increases.

Do not know Jesus only with your mind—meet Him in His Word. Study His life, His nature, and His character, because the better you know Jesus, the more you will become like Him.
