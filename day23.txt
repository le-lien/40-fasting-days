

Bring Your Words Under the Lordship of Jesus Christ

> “Death and life are in the power of the tongue, and those who love it will eat its fruit.”
Proverbs 18:21


🎯 Assignment of the Day

Today, you will intentionally use one of the most powerful weapons in spiritual warfare: your mouth. The enemy seeks to influence your thinking through lies and tempt you to give those lies power by speaking them. But God created your mouth to release truth, faith, blessing, and life.

Begin your day by dedicating your mouth to Jesus. Ask the Holy Spirit to reveal every careless, destructive, fearful, or unbelieving word before you speak it.

Today, stand guard over your words. Carefully examine everything you say. Make a deliberate decision to avoid complaining, grumbling, fear-filled speech, self-condemnation, criticism, gossip, bitterness, negative declarations, and hopeless words. If you catch yourself speaking any of these, immediately renounce them and replace them with God's truth.

Instead, intentionally fill your mouth with the Word of God. Proclaim Scripture aloud over your life, your family, your workplace, your church, and every situation where you are facing spiritual battles. Remember: your circumstances do not have the final word—God's promises do.

Use your words today to strengthen and encourage others. Intentionally bless at least three people through encouragement, prayer, or by sharing a Scripture that points them to Christ. Wherever God's truth is spoken, darkness loses its influence and His light is released.

Pay close attention to the words you speak throughout the day. Before saying something significant, ask yourself:

Does this agree with God's truth?

Does this glorify Jesus?

Does this build faith or feed fear?

Am I agreeing with God's Word or with the enemy's lies?


End the day with a time of proclamation. Thank Jesus for being Lord over your life and declare that your mouth belongs to Him. Ask the Holy Spirit to continually guard your speech so that your words release life, hope, truth, and faith wherever you go.

> Today's Goal: Learn to use your mouth as a spiritual weapon. Do not allow your words to be shaped by your circumstances, but by the truth of God's Word. A heart filled with God's truth will produce words that bring life, strengthen faith, and push back the works of darkness.
