DAY 9 – FASTING FOR THE FAST

Why is fasting so important?

Fasting is not simply giving up food.

Fasting is a spiritual decision to give God more room than our own desires, feelings, habits, and comfort.

When we fast, we are saying:

«"Lord, You are more important than my hunger, my routines, and my own will."»

---

Spiritual Truths About Fasting

☐ Fasting humbles the flesh

The flesh always wants comfort, control, and immediate satisfaction.

Fasting says:

«"My desires will not rule me. God will rule me."»

Fasting brings our heart back under God's authority.

---

☐ Fasting makes us sensitive to God's voice

God is always speaking.

But we are often distracted by noise, schedules, entertainment, and daily pressures.

Fasting removes many of those distractions and helps us hear God more clearly.

---

☐ Fasting reveals what is inside us

During fasting, hidden things often rise to the surface:

- Fear
- Anger
- Pride
- Worry
- Unforgiveness
- Control

God is not exposing these things to condemn us.

He is exposing them to heal us.

---

☐ Fasting creates room for God's presence

Less distraction.

More prayer.

More worship.

More time with God.

Fasting creates space for the Holy Spirit to work deeply in our lives.

---

☐ Fasting strengthens the spirit

The body becomes weaker.

The spirit becomes stronger.

We learn to live by God's Word instead of our emotions and cravings.

---

☐ Fasting resets our priorities

Life constantly pulls our attention toward:

- Work
- Money
- Responsibilities
- Problems
- Entertainment

Fasting reminds us:

«God is my source.»

Not food.

Not success.

Not people.

Not possessions.

---

☐ Fasting brings spiritual clarity

Many believers experience:

- Fresh revelation
- Greater wisdom
- Clear direction
- New understanding

Not because fasting is magic,

but because fasting positions us to listen.

---

☐ Fasting is spiritual warfare

Jesus fasted.

Moses fasted.

Elijah fasted.

Daniel fasted.

The early church fasted.

Fasting draws us closer to God.

And where God's light increases, darkness loses its influence.

---

☐ Fasting does not change God — it changes us

This may be the most important truth.

Fasting does not force God to act.

Fasting prepares our hearts to receive what God desires to do.

---

What do we want to preserve?

☐ Our closeness with God

☐ Our love for prayer

☐ Our hunger for God's Word

☐ A clean and surrendered heart

☐ The freedom we have received

☐ The revelations God has given

☐ The transformation that has begun

☐ The work of the Holy Spirit in our lives

---

What should we pray today?

☐ Lord, preserve the fruit of this fast.

☐ Lord, give me strength to finish well.

☐ Lord, keep me from returning to old patterns.

☐ Lord, increase my hunger for You.

☐ Lord, teach me how to walk with You after the fast is over.

☐ Lord, let this fast become a turning point in my life.

---

The Greatest Revelation

Fasting is not the goal.

Jesus is the goal.

Fasting is only the path.

The less room my flesh has,

the more room God's Spirit has.

And that is where real transformation begins.🔥 Key Thought for Day 9:

Fasting is not about getting more from God.
It is about giving God more of us.
